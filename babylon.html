The Babylonian Empire

The Babylonian civilization was a prominent Mesopotamian culture that emerged in the 18th century BCE. Famous for its advances in law, astronomy, and architecture, it flourished under rulers like Hammurabi and Nebuchadnezzar II. Babylon was home to the famous Hanging Gardens and the Ishtar Gate.

Notable Artifacts

Code of Hammurabi

A basalt stele inscribed with one of the earliest and most complete written legal codes.

Ishtar Gate

A beautifully decorated gate that served as the ceremonial entrance to Babylon.

Hanging Gardens

A legendary series of terraced gardens, considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

Scientific and Artistic Achievements

Astronomy

The Babylonians developed highly accurate astronomical observations and created the zodiac system.

Mathematics

Advanced mathematical concepts, including quadratic equations and the Pythagorean theorem.

Architecture

Known for monumental structures like the Ishtar Gate and ziggurats.
